Strength

Training

Build muscle, improve endurance, and enhance physical power with strength training routines designed for all levels. Each workout focuses on major muscle groups for maximum efficiency.

Duration: 30–45 minutes | Rounds: 3–4 rounds | Rest: 60–90 seconds

Warm-Up

(5-10 Minutes)

Begin your strength training session with a dynamic warm-up to activate your muscles and improve mobility:

  • Arm Circles: 1 minute forward, 1 minute backward
  • Bodyweight Squats: 15 reps
  • Hip Bridges: 10 reps
  • Plank Shoulder Taps: 10 reps per side

Main Strength Training Circuit

Perform each exercise with controlled movements and proper form. Rest for 60–90 seconds between exercises. Repeat the circuit 3–4 times.

01

Push-Ups
(10–15 reps)

  • Target: Chest, shoulders, and triceps.
  • Tips:
    • Keep your body straight from head to heels.
    • Lower your chest to just above the ground and push back up.

02

Squats
(15–20 reps)

  • Target: Quads, hamstrings, and glutes.
  • Tips:
    • Keep your chest lifted and weight on your heels.
    • Go as low as comfortable while maintaining form.

03

Dumbbell Rows
(10–12 reps per arm)

  • Target: Upper back, shoulders, and arms.
  • Tips:
    • Keep your back flat and core engaged.
    • Pull the dumbbell toward your waist, squeezing your shoulder blades together.

04

Deadlifts
(10–15 reps)

  • Target: Hamstrings, glutes, and lower back.
  • Tips:
    • Keep your back neutral and chest up.
    • Hinge at your hips and lift with controlled movements.

05

Plank Hold
(30–60 seconds)

  • Target: Core, shoulders, and stability.
  • Tips:
    • Keep your body in a straight line.
    • Engage your core and avoid letting your hips sag.

Cool Down

(5 Minutes)

End your workout with these essential stretches to relax your muscles:

  • Standing Hamstring Stretch: 30 seconds per leg
  • Child's Pose: 1 minute
  • Chest Opener: 30 seconds
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: 5 cycles

Progression Over Time

Gradually increase intensity to ensure continuous improvement and avoid plateaus. Adjust your workout over time as follows:

  • Weeks 1–2: Focus on mastering form and completing the circuit for 3 rounds. Stick to the recommended rep range.
  • Weeks 3–4: Increase to 4 rounds or add 2–3 extra reps per exercise (e.g., Push-Ups from 10–15 to 12–18 reps).
  • Weeks 5–6: Introduce light weights (e.g., dumbbells) for exercises like Squats and Dumbbell Rows. Extend Plank Hold by 10–15 seconds.
  • Beyond Week 6: Gradually add resistance, increase reps or rounds, or incorporate advanced variations (e.g., Weighted Push-Ups, Jump Squats).
