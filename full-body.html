Full-Body

Circuit

Duration: 30–45 minutes | Rounds: 3–4 rounds |

Rest: 1–2 minutes between rounds

Warm-Up (5 Minutes)

Before starting, perform a quick warm-up to activate muscles and prevent injuries:

  • Jumping Jacks: 2 minutes
  • Arm Circles: 30 seconds forward, 30 seconds backward
  • Bodyweight Squats: 10 reps
  • Hip Openers: 10 reps per leg

Main Circuit

Perform each exercise in sequence with minimal rest between exercises. Complete the circuit 3–4 times.

01

Push-Ups
(10–15 reps)

  • Target Muscles: Chest, shoulders, triceps, and core.
  • Form:
    • Place hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.
    • Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    • Lower your body until your chest is close to the ground, then push back up.
  • Modification: Perform knee push-ups or incline push-ups (hands on a sturdy elevated surface) if needed.

02

Squats
(15–20 reps)

  • Target Muscles: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves.
  • Form:
    • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
    • Lower your body as if sitting in a chair, keeping your chest up and knees tracking over toes.
    • Go as low as comfortable and push back to standing.
  • Progression: Hold a weight (e.g., a filled backpack) for added resistance.

03

Lunges
(10–15 reps /leg)

  • Target Muscles: Quads, hamstrings, and glutes.
  • Form:
    • Step forward with one leg, lowering your back knee toward the ground.
    • Keep your front knee over your ankle and maintain an upright posture.
    • Push through your front heel to return to the starting position.
  • Progression: Add resistance or perform jumping lunges for intensity.

04

Plank
(30–60 seconds)

  • Target Muscles: Core (abs, lower back), shoulders, and glutes.
  • Form:
    • Place forearms on the ground, elbows under shoulders.
    • Keep your body straight from head to heels.
    • Hold the position, engaging your core muscles.
  • Modification: Drop knees to the floor to reduce intensity.

05

Burpees
(10 reps)

  • Target Muscles: Full body (cardio, strength, and agility).
  • Form:
    • Start standing, drop into a squat position with hands on the floor.
    • Jump feet back into a plank position.
    • Perform a push-up (optional), then jump feet forward and leap into the air.
  • Modification: Skip the push-up or jump for a low-impact version.

Rest (1–2 Minutes)

Take a short rest between rounds to recover. Use this time to sip water and stretch briefly.

Cool Down (5 Minutes)

Finish with stretching to improve flexibility and aid recovery:

  • Hamstring Stretch: 30 seconds per leg
  • Quad Stretch: 30 seconds per leg
  • Child’s Pose: 1 minute
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: 5 cycles

Progression Over Time

  • Weeks 1–2: Stick to the rep range and 3 rounds.
  • Weeks 3–4: Increase reps (e.g., Push-Ups to 20 reps) or rounds (4 rounds total).
  • Add weights or resistance bands for more challenge as you progress.
